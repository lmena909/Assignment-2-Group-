Who: Newcomer children and youth, ages anywhere between 7 to 18, are more than welcome to join this program.

What: The program offers a mix of indoor activities and outdoor sports-based activities which will be designed to promote health, belonging, and community connection.

Where: Based in Calgary, Alberta, the program operates through community centers, parks, and recreational facilities.

When: Full-day, structured programming runs weekly from last June through August for 5 weeks.

Why: Our mission is to help newcomer children and youth feel seen, heard, supported, and valued.

Our Services

  • Youth summer programs(ages between 7–18)
  • Cultural integration activities
  • Extended Support for newcomers
  • Language support activities

Weekly Structure Overview

Each week a 6-7-hour session will be held which will focused on a different theme. This program will run for 5 weeks, in which diverse activities that align with the program's goals: health, integration, and community connection.

Weekly scheduling Monday-Friday for 5 weeks

Week Time Activity Details
Week 1 10:00am – 5:00pm Introduction & Icebreaker Activities
  • Team-building games and goal setting
  • Create and decorate notebooks and name tags
  • Guided painting session
  • Light outdoor games (Example: frisbee, relay races)
Week 2 10:00am – 5:00pm Arts & Crafts
  • Painting or pottery with a local artist
  • Creative crafts: jewelry, rock painting, tie-dye
  • Organized sports and outdoor games
  • Group picnic
Week 3 Field Trip: Glenbow Ranch or Calgary Zoo (All-Day Event)
Week 4 10:00am – 5:00pm Crafting Week
  • Clay modeling and sculpture activities
  • DIY activities
Week 5 10:00am – 5:00pm Guest Speaker: Indigenous Storytelling & Art
  • Storytelling session on Indigenous history and culture
  • Art project: beadword and creating cultural symbols
  • Traditional Indigenous games
